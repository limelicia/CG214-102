Wat Arun Ratchawararam Ratchawaramahawihan (Wat Arun)

About Wat Arun

Wat Arun Ratchawararam Ratchawaramahawihan was also known as Wat Arun, or the "Temple of Dawn". It is a Buddhist temple in Bangkok, Thailand. The temple got its name from the Hindu god, Aruna, who represents the rising sun. It is one of Thailand's famous landmarks. The first ray of light from the morning Sun reflects off the temple's surface, giving it a pearly iridescence appearance. Wat Arun can be recognised by its distinctive prang, which was built in the early nineteenth century.

Interesting Facts of Wat Arun

  • Originally known as Wat Makok, named after its situated village, Bang Makok
  • Wat Makok had existed since the time of the Ayutthaya Kingdom
  • Wat Makok was shown in French maps in the mid 17th century
  • After the fall of the Ayutthaya Kingdom, King Taksin renamed Wat Makok as Wat Chaeng
  • King Taksin was presumed to have vowed to restore the temple after passing it at dawn
